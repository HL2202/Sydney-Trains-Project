Covid-19 Delays: Find Out Which Train Lines Are Most Affected

Harry Li July 05, 2020 painting, work tools, tags 0 comments

As restrictions begin to ease from July 1, new data analytics into travel times have revealed which train lines were delayed the most often in the past financial year.

Trains have been running on-time more often over the past financial year, indicating improved services and better station managements, but are also a prediction for how the services might change ahead of the new lifting of non-essential travel bans.

"The services have continued to run and produce some excellent results month by month", says Chris Minns, Shadow Minister for Transport.

7.5% of all trains were over 5 minutes late in the past financial year. However, the month before lockdown due to Covid-19 recorded 17% of trains to be running late.

"Our great fear is that as people return to work and as social distancing requirements disappear, we'll go back to train chaos on the network", warns Mr Minns.

Last financial year's data revealed the most delayed train line was the T1 North Shore Line, with over 10% of all trains running at least 5 minutes late.

Source: Seven News Australia

Following the North Shore Line was the T9 Northern Line, with 9% of all trains that arrived 5 minutes later than the scheduled time.

Source: Seven News Australia

Delays were also noticeable on the T2 Inner West (7.9%), T3 Bankstown (7.5%), T8 Airport/South (6.9%), and T4 East/Illawarra (4.8%).

Source: Seven News Australia

While the overall decrease in trains running late are a good indication for most travellers, the unprecedented circumstances associated with Covid-19 could see a greater number of trains being delayed unless more services are added, and there are fears that another wave of the pandemic could surface when life returns to normal. Overall, the changes on trains due to social distancing continue to be heavily monitored.

Transport NSW says it will review timetables to improve efficiency.
