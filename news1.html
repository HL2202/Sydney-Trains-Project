Travel Restrictions Lifted: Preparing For Your Next Trip

Harry Li June 10, 2020 painting, work tools, tags 0 comments

After the non-essential travel ban was lifted on Monday June 1, people across Sydney are planning for their future trips, whether it is a return to work, visiting family and friends, or going back to school. This article helps you understand the CovidSafe Transport Plan and the travel times you should expect.

Over 250 new weekly train services have been added to help commuters continue to make smart travel choices with more options provided, and to temporarily increase capacity to practice physical distancing.

The CovidSafe Transport Plan will create 60,000 extra spaces on trains across the network weekly to support children travelling to school and people returning to work. The increased capacity is designed for off-peak travel.

Trains are now cleaned up to four times a day.

There have been temporary changes to peak and off-peak times in the past two weeks, with off-peak times from 10am to 2pm. This is intended to provide more information and adaptable options throughout the day and help flatten the travel peak curve.


Green Dots

Green dots have been placed throughout the train to reflect the capacity of each carriage.

Follow physical distancing guidance - 'look for the green dots'

Distinctive green dots have been placed on public transport to indicate the safe areas for sitting and standing. If capacity has reached maximum, you may be asked to wait for the next service, with school children and those requiring assistance given priority access.

Source: https://transportnsw.info/covid-19

The following guidelines will explain physical distancing on public transport:

1. Capacity restrictions have been put in place. Please check the capacity before boarding.

2. From July 1, more green dots will be placed to reflect an increase in capacity.

3. Maintain 1.5 metre physical distancing across the network; on trains and on the platform.

The figures below show the number of seats (seated and standing capacity) per travel mode:

Sydney Trains

Waratah - 68 per carriage (T1, T2, T3, T5, T8, T9)

Millennium - 72 per carriage (T1, T2, T3, T5, T8, T9)

Tangara - 66 per carriage (T1, T2, T3, T4, T5, T8, T9)

C-set - 63 per carriage (T2, T3, T5, T8)

K-set - 60 per carriage (T2, T3, T5, T7, T8)


NSW TrainLink

Oscar - 70 per carriage (T1, T4, T9, CCN, SCO)

V-set - 58 per carriage (BMT, CCN)

Hunter - 46 per carriage (HUN)

Endeavour - 47 per carriage (BMT, HUN, SCO, SHL)

XPT - 54 per service (North Coast NSW, Western NSW, Southern NSW)

Xplorer - 90 per service (North Western NSW, Western NSW, Southern NSW)

Coaches - 20 per bus

Source: https://transportnsw.info/covid-19


What This Means For Your Next Journey

As you prepare to board trains again, it is important to always remember that you should plan your journey in advance and account for any delays. While these increased services will help to reduce the amount of congestion in train stations, it might not necessarily help all services continue to run on-time frequently, because physical distancing and carriage capacity provides a new challenge for both commuters and rail staff. As the number of travellers are still expected to be lower compared to before lockdown, the amount of delays during off-peak times should be predicted to decrease.
