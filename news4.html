Students Return To School: Helping Maintain Physical Distancing On Trains

Harry Li May 28, 2020 painting, work tools, tags 3 comments

From Monday 25 May, students are returning to school for face-to-face lessons. This means many students across the state will be taking public transport from Monday to Friday every week. This article provides a guide for both students and adults on how to maintain physical distancing on trains.

As a traveller, you are encouraged to:

1. Practice good hygiene by regularly washing hands and using hand sanitisers to protect yourself and others.

2. Cover your sneezes and coughs to avoid transmission.

3. Avoid close contact with other people at stations and on trains.

4. Stay home if you are feeling unwell.

5. Travel outside of peak times where possible

Green dots have been placed in every train carriage to indicate the safest places to sit and stand. There is now extra cleaning for high touch areas, including Opal card readers and hand rails on trains. The availability of each service will be indicated on the station screens. Adults are encouraged to wait for the next service if the train capacity is full to help maintain physical distancing.

Source: https://transportnsw.info/covid-19

School students will be given priority access to all modes of public transport to ensure they are able to arrive at school on time. The NSW Government's policy is to not leave a school student behind. Students are reminded to tap on and tap off using their Opal cards on Opal enabled services.

During the past weeks of lockdown, the number of trains running on-time have significantly increased, however this may change as physical distancing and carriage capacity are introduced. To prevent over-crowding of train stations, train services will run more often. This minimises the need for passengers to wait longer for a train if one service is full.

Students across the state are encouraged to maintain physical distancing from other students and passengers while on-board trains and on platforms. This is to reduce any risks of spreading or contracting the Covid-19. As students are given priority onto trains, this could lead to greater waiting times during peak hours or the risk of a service going over physical distancing capacity. Therefore, more transport staff are deployed to assist students on their journey to school.

Delay times are predicted to increase as an increasing number of people begin taking public transport when restrictions ease. The capacity of the trains may have to increase to support the extra commuters, whilst more staff may be needed to monitor train stations to ensure all trains arrive and depart on time.
