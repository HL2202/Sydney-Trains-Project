Off-Peak Times Changed: How This Will Affect Your Travel

Harry Li July 16, 2020 painting, work tools, tags 0 comments

The next stage of the easing of restrictions in NSW on Wednesday July 1 gave the green light to travel resumption on public transport. However, the NSW government is changing the definition of off-peak travel.

As more commuters return to Sydney Trains, additional services were added outside of peak times to allow for the increase in the network capacity, with extra staff and cleaning for public transport and new sanitisation cleaning stations.

The new definition of off-peak are times when few people want or need to travel. Sydney's former peak hours were 7am to 9am in the morning peak and 4pm to 6:30pm in the evening peak. Now, the peak hours are 6:30am to 10am and 3pm to 7pm, reducing three hours from the off-peak times.

Source: Seven News Australia

As the times of peak hours change, and new data shows delays were significantly higher the month before lockdown,this could lead to an increase in trains running late for the new peak hours because these are times recorded to have the most commuters travelling according to the NSW government.

Furthermore, the easing of restrictions has already been proven to be a challenge for Sydney Trains, as extra services have been continually added to account for the increasing number of people using public transport, and there are predictions that even more trains will need to run as more people return to work.

However, there has been a fare hike associated with the new changing of peak times, which are not a result of Covid-19, but will be put in place until at least the next year. The government is offering a 50% discount for off-peak travel, which is intended to encourage off-peak travel.

Travelling from Penrith to Circular Quay used to cost $4.82 off-peak, now it will cost $6.89 and count as a peak time.

Source: Seven News Australia

The 50% discount for off-peak travel will also be reduced in 3 months. The Sunday Funday $2.50 fare for unlimited travel has also been removed.

More commuters could now be expected to travel in the off-peak times during these 3 months.
